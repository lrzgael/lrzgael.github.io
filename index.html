Hello ! I am Gaël Le Ruz, a postdoctoral researcher in applied mathematics. I am currently working at Institut de Mathématiques de Toulouse on the design of an optimal control of intermittent aeration for an activated sludge wastewater treatment plant, in collaboration with Jérôme Fehrenbach.

I did my PhD on optimal filtering theory in manifolds from a deterministic perspective, under the supervision of Philippe Moireau and Damiano Lombardi.. I was part of the Laboratoire Jacques-Louis Lions, and worked within the COMMEDIA and ANANKE Inria teams.

Research

My research focuses on the design and analysis of original methods for data assimilation and optimal control problems. Within the field of data assimilation, I am particularly interested in observer-based methods, which can be formulated through an optimal control lens.

I am also dedicated to applying these techniques to real-world scenarios, particularly for environmental sciences. Consequently, an important part of my research is devoted to the development and analysis of the underlying numerical methods.
